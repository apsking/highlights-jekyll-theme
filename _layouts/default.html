    The Couple

    Kimberly and Alex have been solving problems together since day one. They met in Multivariate Calculus class at the University of Puget Sound in 2011. They were great study partners, often meeting up for breakfast before class to help each other with difficult homework problems and studying for tests together.

    When they returned to school in January 2012 after winter break, they were greeted with a rare 8 inches of snow in Tacoma. The university declared a snow day, and after a fun-filled day of playing in the snow with friends, Alex and Kimberly shared their first kiss under the falling snow on Puget Sound’s campus.

    Throughout college, they supported one another through their different areas of study and took breaks to attend formals and explore the Tacoma waterfront. After graduation, they moved 40 miles north to Seattle to start their careers, still enjoying the close company of many college friends.

    In August 2018, Alex and Kimberly made the big move to St. Louis, MO for Kimberly to attend Saint Louis University’s PhD program in Clinical Psychology, while Alex continued to work remotely for Amazon. They were thrilled to adopt a black-lab mix puppy into their home in March 2019, who they named Rainier to honor their Pacific Northwest roots. Little “Rainy” has happily settled into the routine of napping throughout Alex’s work days and greeting Kimberly with a wagging tail when she gets home every day.

    About seven years after they shared their first snowy kiss, on the eve before 2019, Alex and Kimberly found yet another romantic snowy setting. The two set out for a hike to Franklin Falls in the Cascade Mountains outside of Seattle, trekking through sparkling snow to the foot of the waterfall. As they stood admiring the magnificent cascade, Alex dropped to one knee and proposed to Kimberly. Once Kimberly enthusiastically accepted his proposal, he pointed out Kimberly’s parents, Doug and Maureen, who had been huddled in the cold to document the moment. They enjoyed a quick celebration at the waterfall, then hiked back to the warmth to share the news with friends and family.

    What can I do in the area?

    The City - Portland, OR

    Welcome to Portland, Oregon's largest city, where the locals like to keep it weird! It has so many things to do that visitors can experience something new every time they visit. Some of our favorites include strolling in the Pearl District, where you can find the world's largest books store, Powell's Books. Check out our family-favorite brewery, Breakside Brewing, or Great Notion Brewing for some absolutely incredible brews! While many tourists flock to Voodoo Donuts for its quirky sweet treats, many locals prefer Blue Star, where you'll find a more elegant take on donuts! There are way too many things to list that make Portland awesome. Consider staying a few days to see all that it has to offer!

    The Outdoors - Bend, OR

    Trying to experience the great Oregon outdoors? Bend, OR is perfect for you! Oregon's high desert has endless ways to experience its beauty - from hiking and cycling, to its family-friendly resorts, to the Deschutes River. Bend is famous for its number of craft breweries, who offer some incredible beer - don't be scared off by its name, Boneyard Brew Pub is one of our favorites! Another absolute gem is Crux!

    The River - Hood River, OR

    Looking for some amazing food and outdoors right in Portland's backyard? Head out to the Columbia River Gorge. Experience the 620-foot Multnomah Falls on your way to Hood River, where you can take in some breathtaking scenery, boutique hotels, and great beer! Check out Pfriem Beer or Full Sail Brewing for some tasty beer right along the river!

    The Beach - Pacific City, OR

    If breathing in the sea air is more your style, Pacific City is perfect for you! Enjoy some local beer with your feet in the sand at Pelican Brewing and then take a short walk on the beach to climb the local dunes at Cape Kiwanda State Natural Area. Consider taking a drive up the coast to the Tillamook Creamery for some famous ice cream and cheese or drive south to play in the world-famous Oregon Dunes.

    Guests may begin arriving at 3:15pm. We recommend that you arrive no later than 3:45pm to give you enough time to park, walk to the event lawn, be greeted, and take your seats before the ceremony begins at 4:00pm.

    On the day of our wedding, we are going for dressy casual attire. June in Oregon may bring rain or shine, so you can scroll up to see the weather forecast in the area. Our ceremony and reception will be held outdoors. If rain is in the forecast, we will hold the ceremony under the covering of a tent. Regarding footwear, there is a gravel pathway to the ceremony and reception lawn, and we will be on grass most of the evening.
