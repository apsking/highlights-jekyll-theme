    The Couple

    Kimberly and Alex have been solving problems together since day one. They met in Multivariate Calculus class at the University of Puget Sound in 2011. They were great study partners, often meeting up for breakfast before class to help each other with difficult homework problems and studying for tests together.

    When they returned to school in January 2012 after winter break, they were greeted with a rare 8 inches of snow in Tacoma. The university declared a snow day, and after a fun-filled day of playing in the snow with friends, Alex and Kimberly shared their first kiss under the falling snow on Puget Sound’s campus.

    Throughout college, they supported one another through their different areas of study and took breaks to attend formals and explore the Tacoma waterfront. After graduation, they moved 40 miles north to Seattle to start their careers, still enjoying the close company of many college friends.

    In August 2018, Alex and Kimberly made the big move to St. Louis, MO for Kimberly to attend Saint Louis University’s PhD program in Clinical Psychology, while Alex continued to work remotely for Amazon. They were thrilled to adopt a black-lab mix puppy into their home in March 2019, who they named Rainier to honor their Pacific Northwest roots. Little “Rainy” has happily settled into the routine of napping throughout Alex’s work days and greeting Kimberly with a wagging tail when she gets home every day.

    About seven years after they shared their first snowy kiss, on the eve before 2019, Alex and Kimberly found yet another romantic snowy setting. The two set out for a hike to Franklin Falls in the Cascade Mountains outside of Seattle, trekking through sparkling snow to the foot of the waterfall. As they stood admiring the magnificent cascade, Alex dropped to one knee and proposed to Kimberly. Once Kimberly enthusiastically accepted his proposal, he pointed out Kimberly’s parents, Doug and Maureen, who had been huddled in the cold to document the moment. They enjoyed a quick celebration at the waterfall, then hiked back to the warmth to share the news with friends and family.
